An Astronomer’s perspective on Deep Learning

This blog contains notes and notebooks of a professional Astronomer diving deep into the study of deep learning. What I found during my quest, and what I report here, is meant to be purely anecdotical and not necessarily following a logical and sound educational path. The hope with which I build this blog is that it will eventually be useful to me to look back at and that other people with a similar background may find some use out of these notes and notebooks.

My name is Lorenzo Posti and I’m currently a postdoctoral researcher at the Strasbourg Observatory (France) specialized in models of galaxy dynamics, which I use to study the origin of galaxies and the nature of dark matter. Find my personal research page here

This site is built using fastpages. The diagram of the neural network in the banner was made using NN-SVG, the input image shows isophotal contours of the galaxy NGC 4342 rendered from Hubble observations.
